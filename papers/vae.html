Auto-Encoding Variational Bayes (VAE)

Reference: Kingma & Welling, 2013

Abstract

Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) provide a probabilistic manner for describing an observation in latent space. Rather than mapping inputs to fixed vectors like standard autoencoders, a VAE maps inputs to parameters of a probability distribution (mean and variance), enabling the generation of new, continuous data.

Methodology

I built a VAE using PyTorch, featuring an MLP-based encoder and decoder. The network was trained on the MNIST dataset.

  • Latent Dimension: 20
  • Optimizer: Adam (lr=1e-3)
  • Loss Function: Binary Cross-Entropy + Kullback-Leibler (KL) Divergence
