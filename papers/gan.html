Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)

Goodfellow et al., 2014 • PyTorch Implementation

Abstract

This project reproduces the foundational work on Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). A framework is established where two models are trained simultaneously: a generative model G that captures the data distribution, and a discriminative model D that estimates the probability that a sample came from the training data rather than G. The training procedure for G is to maximize the probability of D making a mistake.

Implementation Details

The models were implemented in PyTorch using standard Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) to mirror the architecture described in the original paper for the MNIST dataset. The generator utilizes ReLU activations with a final Tanh layer, while the discriminator uses LeakyReLU and a final Sigmoid activation. 

# Discriminator architecture snippet
nn.Sequential(
    nn.Linear(784, 512),
    nn.LeakyReLU(0.2, inplace=True),
    nn.Linear(512, 256),
    nn.LeakyReLU(0.2, inplace=True),
    nn.Linear(256, 1),
    nn.Sigmoid()
)

Hardware & Training

Training was conducted locally on an RTX 2050 GPU. Due to the lightweight nature of the MLPs and the use of the MNIST dataset, the training process was efficient and completed within minutes.

Results

The generator successfully learned the underlying distribution of the MNIST dataset, producing recognizable handwritten digits from random noise vectors in the latent space. Early epochs showed noisy artifacts, which steadily resolved into distinct numeral shapes as the adversarial training stabilized.
