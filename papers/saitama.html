Kinetic Energy of Saitama's Punch

Theoretical Anime Physics Department, 2024

Abstract

This paper calculates the kinetic energy output required for the "Serious Punch" capable of parting the Earth's atmosphere on a global scale. By analyzing the displaced air mass and the velocity of the shockwave, we estimate the joules expended and discuss the biomechanical implications of such force generation.

Methodology

To split the cloud cover across a hemisphere, a massive pressure wave must be generated. We model the atmosphere as a compressible fluid and calculate the energy required to displace a volume of air $V$ equivalent to the cleared area. 


def calculate_punch_energy(cloud_clearance_radius_km, shockwave_velocity_mps):
    # Standard atmospheric density at sea level
    rho_air = 1.225 # kg/m^3

    # Volume of a hemisphere of air
    radius_m = cloud_clearance_radius_km * 1000
    volume = (2/3) * 3.14159 * (radius_m ** 3)

    # Mass of the displaced air
    mass_air = rho_air * volume

    # Kinetic energy (E = 1/2 * m * v^2)
    energy_joules = 0.5 * mass_air * (shockwave_velocity_mps ** 2)
    return energy_joules

Results

Assuming a clearance radius of 5,000 kilometers and a shockwave velocity approaching a significant fraction of $c$ to overcome atmospheric pressure instantaneously, the energy output exceeds $10^{28}$ Joules. This is orders of magnitude greater than the Chicxulub impactor (the asteroid that caused the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event).

Conclusion

The kinetic energy of a "Serious Punch" reaches planetary-busting levels. The survival of the Earth's crust following the recoil of such an attack suggests either directed energy transfer with 100% efficiency or a localized violation of Newton's Third Law.
