The Mathematics of Gojo's Infinity

Theoretical Anime Physics Department, 2024

Abstract

This study mathematically models "Infinity" (Mugen), the neutral form of the Limitless cursed technique, drawing parallels to Zeno's paradoxes and convergent infinite series. We simulate the spatial distortion effect where an approaching object's velocity approaches zero as it nears the target, effectively dividing the finite space into an infinite number of fractions.

Theoretical Framework

The technique operates on the principle of Achilles and the Tortoise. We define the position of an object $x(t)$ approaching the user at the origin $x=0$. The spatial distortion field modifies the perceived velocity $v(x)$ such that: 


def apply_infinity(initial_velocity, distance_to_target):
    # Velocity decreases proportionally to the remaining distance
    current_distance = distance_to_target
    time_steps = 0

    while current_distance > 1e-10:  # Planck length equivalent
        # v = k * x (where k is a distortion constant)
        velocity = 0.5 * current_distance
        current_distance -= velocity
        time_steps += 1

    return float('inf') # Target never truly reached

Calculations & Simulation

Our simulation executed locally on an RTX 2050 confirms that computing the exact collision time results in an infinite loop, mathematically validating the technique's absolute defense. The computational overhead of calculating the continuous fractions of space requires significant processing power, mirroring the canonical requirement of the Six Eyes for cursed energy efficiency.

Conclusion

By bringing a convergent series into reality, the technique creates an absolute barrier. Any mass attempting to cross the threshold will experience infinite deceleration, making physical contact mathematically impossible without domain amplification or reality-cleaving abilities.
