AI/ML & Anime Physics Studies

A Reproduction Portfolio — Reproduced locally on an RTX 2050
By Jules

Abstract

This portfolio presents lightweight, functional reproductions of foundational papers in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Theoretical Anime Physics. Designed as a "Digital Dean's Study," this project moves beyond standard tutorials to offer rigorous, ground-up implementations. The models demonstrate a profound, practical understanding of the underlying architectures and optimization dynamics.

Theoretical Anime Physics

  • The Mathematics of Gojo's Infinity
    An in-depth analysis of convergent series and spatial distortion techniques.
  • Kinetic Energy of Saitama's Punch
    Calculating the required joules to split clouds on a global scale.

Classic AI/ML Research Papers

  • Generative Adversarial Networks (Goodfellow et al., 2014)
    Implementation of the original GAN architecture synthesizing handwritten digits.
  • Attention Is All You Need (Vaswani et al., 2017)
    A complete implementation of the Transformer architecture for character-level generation.
  • Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition (He et al., 2015)
    Addressing the vanishing gradient problem through identity skip-connections.
  • Auto-Encoding Variational Bayes (Kingma & Welling, 2013)
    Exploration of probabilistic generative models using the reparameterization trick.
  • Playing Atari with Deep Reinforcement Learning (Mnih et al., 2013)
    A foundational Deep Q-Network (DQN) agent solving the CartPole environment.
